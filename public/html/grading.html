Authentication & Grading

Card Grading

Getting your cards professionally graded protects your investment, verifies authenticity, and can dramatically increase resale value. Here's what you need to know about the major grading companies.

Why Grade?

The Value of a Slab

A graded card is examined by experts, sealed in a tamper-evident slab, and assigned a numeric grade. That grade is a permanent, verifiable record that follows the card forever — buyers trust slabs, and the market prices them accordingly.

Increased Value

A PSA 9 or 10 can be worth multiples of a raw copy. Even mid-grades often sell for a premium over ungraded cards.

Verified Authenticity

Every submission is inspected for alterations, trimming, and counterfeiting. A graded card is a card buyers can trust.

Protected Forever

The rigid, UV-resistant slab protects your card from bending, moisture, and handling damage indefinitely.

Easier to Sell

Graded cards sell faster online. Condition is already settled — no disputes, no returns over condition disagreements.

The Major Graders

Grading Companies

Each grading company has its own reputation, turnaround times, pricing structure, and collector following. The right choice depends on your card type, budget, and where you plan to sell.

PSA
Professional Sports Authenticator — the most recognized name in the hobby. PSA slabs command the highest premiums on the secondary market, especially for sports cards. Grades 1–10.
Submit to PSA
BGS / Beckett
Beckett Grading Services uses a subgrade system (centering, corners, edges, surface) and awards half-point grades. Their Black Label 10 (all 10 subgrades) is one of the most coveted grades in the hobby.
Submit to BGS
SGC
Sportscard Guaranty is known for faster turnaround and competitive pricing. SGC's clean slab design is popular with vintage collectors, and their grades carry real weight for pre-war and pre-1980 cards.
Submit to SGC
CGC
CGC Cards is the go-to for trading card games (Pokémon, Magic: The Gathering, Yu-Gi-Oh) but also grades sports cards. Great option if you're mixing TCG cards in with sports submissions.
Submit to CGC

General Process

How to Submit

The process is similar across all companies. Here's what to expect:

01

Create an Account

Register on the grading company's website. Most require an account to place an order and track your submission.

02

Choose a Service Level

Each company offers multiple tiers based on card value and desired turnaround. Economy tiers are cheaper but slower; express tiers cost more but grade faster.

03

Package & Ship

Sleeve each card, secure them between team bags or cardboard, and ship with tracking and insurance. Each company publishes specific packaging guidelines on their site.

04

Track & Receive

Monitor your submission status online. When grading is complete your slabs ship back to you with a grade report.
